Brushtail Possum

The brushtail possum is a nocturnal marsupial native to Australia that has become one of the country's most successful invasive species in New Zealand. These arboreal creatures are highly adaptable and can thrive in diverse environments ranging from wet forests to urban areas. Known for their intelligence and curiosity, brushtail possums have thick fur ranging in color from silver-grey to almost black, and possess a distinctive bushy tail.

  • Lifespan: 10 - 12 years (In the wild)
  • Scientific name: Trichosurus vulpecula
  • Mass: 1.6 - 4.5 kg (Adult)
  • Body length: 32 - 58 cm (Adult)
  • Diet: Brushtail possums are herbivorous, feeding primarily on leaves, bark, fruits, and flowers from a wide variety of trees and shrubs.
  • Habitat: Native to Australian forests and woodlands, they have successfully colonized New Zealand where they are considered an invasive pest species threatening native vegetation.
